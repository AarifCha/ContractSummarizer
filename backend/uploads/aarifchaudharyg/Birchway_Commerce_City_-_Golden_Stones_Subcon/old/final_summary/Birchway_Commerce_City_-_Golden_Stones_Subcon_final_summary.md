## 1. Top Issues & Executive Summary

This subcontract agreement presents significant risks to the Subcontractor, primarily concerning payment and liability. The contract includes strong "Pay-if-Paid" and "Condition Precedent" clauses, meaning the Subcontractor's payment is directly contingent on the General Contractor receiving payment from the Owner. This shifts substantial payment risk to the Subcontractor. Indemnification obligations are broad, requiring defense and indemnification of multiple parties against a wide range of claims, including those arising from joint negligence and Employee Claims. Schedule adherence is critical, with "Time is of the essence" and a strict "No-Damage-for-Delay" clause, limiting Subcontractor's remedy to time extensions only. Compliance requirements are extensive, particularly regarding safety, OCIP insurance, and the use of the Procore/Textura systems, with non-compliance leading to backcharges, payment withholding, or even termination.
